Udupi Shri Krishna Temple

History

Dedicated to Lord Krishna, it is one of the most revered Temples in the region. The presiding deity is depicted as Bal Krishna. However, unlike other Temples, the idol cannot be seen directly. Devotees can see the idol through a 9-holed window called Navagraha Kitiki. The sacred Sri Krishna Temple and Matta (monastery) date back to the 13th century and attract devotees from far and wide.

Gokarna Mahabaleshwar Temple

History

Gokarna, a city blessed with serene beaches and renowned temples, is a place of spiritual significance. Among its sacred sites, the Gokarna Mahabaleshwar Temple holds a special place, drawing numerous pilgrims. This temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is not only steeped in local lore but also features in the ancient epics, the Ramayana and the Mahabharata. Often referred to as Dakshin Kashi, it is regarded with the same reverence as the holy city of Kashi.

Virupaksha Temple

History

This iconic temple is an integral part of the Group of Monuments at Hampi and is devoted to Lord Shiva, revered as Virupaksha, the divine consort of the goddess Pampa Devi. Unlike many others, this temple remains actively used for worship in Hampi, rendering it a significant pilgrimage destination in the state. The ideal times to explore this temple are during the annual chariot festival in February and the joyous marriage celebrations of Virupaksha and Pampa in December

Murudeshwar Shiva Temple

History

Perched atop the Kanduka hill within the town of Murudeshwar, this temple stands as one of the most renowned in the state. Its iconic 123-feet-tall idol of Lord Shiva is the second-largest in the world. With the Arabian Sea embracing it on three sides, the temple offers awe-inspiring vistas. Adding to its grandeur, the temple boasts a towering Gopuram that soars to an impressive height of 237 feet, ranking among the tallest in India.

History

History

History

Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple

History

The 'Ranganathaswamy Temple,' devoted to Lord Vishnu and situated on Srirangam Island within Tiruchirappalli, Tamil Nadu, stands as an architectural masterpiece in the Dravidian style. Covering an expansive area of more than 150 acres, this temple complex boasts 49 sub-shrines and is adorned with 21 exquisitely crafted Gopurams, or tower gateways. The principal Gopuram, known as the 'RajaGopuram,' proudly soars to a remarkable height of 236 feet, securing its position as the tallest Gopuram in Asia..

Meenakshi Amman Temple

History

The Meenakshi Amman Temple, dedicated to Goddess Parvati in her Meenakshi form and Lord Shiva as Lord Sundareswarar, stands as one of India's most ancient and renowned temples, not limited to Tamil Nadu alone.Historical and archaeological accounts trace its origins back to the 6th century AD, though a significant part of the temple suffered damage during the 14th century due to invasions by Muslim forces in India. The present architectural marvel we see today, restored to its original grandeur, dates back to the 16th century when it was meticulously reconstructed by the Nayak rulers.
