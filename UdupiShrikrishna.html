Shri Krishna Temple Udupi

History

The Krishna Matha, established by the Vaishnavite saint Jagadguru Shri Madhvacharya in the 13th century, is renowned for its connection to Lord Krishna. Madhvacharya is credited as the founder of the Dwaita school of Vedanta. It is believed that Madhvacharya came across the Vigraha (divine idol) of Shri Krishna within a large ball of gopichandana.

As described by Sri Madhvacharya in his Tantrasara Sangraha, the Vigraha is positioned facing west, and this westward orientation is maintained for all the other Vigrahas in the various Ashta Muthas associated with the temple. Devotees have the opportunity to view Lord Krishna through two distinct windows: the inner window, known as the Navagraha Kindi, and the outer window, named after the esteemed saint Kanakadasa, known as the Kanakana Kindi. There is also a statue commemorating this arrangement. It is worth noting that the Navagraha Kindi is sometimes confused with the Kanakana Kindi.

The temple opens its doors at 5:30 hours IST. One of its distinctive features is the worship of the deity through a silver-plated window with nine holes, known as the Navagraha Kindi. Additionally, the temple is well-known for serving prasada (lunch) at noon and is affectionately referred to as Anna Brahma, as it provides nourishment to a large number of devotees.
