The Temple of Timelessness: India's Most Historic Shrine

History

This ancient temple is dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga and holds the distinction of being one of India's oldest operational Hindu temples. It stands as a testament to the rich cultural and religious heritage of the region.

The temple, believed to have originated during the Gupta Empire, likely between the 4th and 5th centuries CE, ranks among India's ancient surviving temples. Its exact origins remain a subject of historical debate. Some scholars attribute its construction to the Gupta Empire, while others contend it could date back to the Mauryan era. For more than 2500 years, the temple's idol has been a focal point of worship, and a remarkable piece of history lies within its grounds—a 3000-year-old tree fossil.

How to Arrive!!!

You can access the temple by road through routes from Patna, Gaya, or Varanasi. The nearest railway station is Mohania - Bhabua Road railway station, which is approximately 22 km away from the temple by road. If you prefer air travel, the nearest airport is Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport in Varanasi, located about 102 km away from the temple.

Shri Jagannath Puri Temple

History

The Jagannath Temple stands as a revered Hindu sanctuary devoted to Lord Jagannath, a manifestation of Lord Vishnu, who is a pivotal figure within the trinity of supreme divinity in Hinduism. Located in the picturesque state of Odisha, along the eastern shoreline of India, this sacred temple's origins are steeped in legend and history.

It was King Indradyumna of Avanti who undertook the monumental task of constructing the primary shrine dedicated to Lord Jagannath in the heart of Puri. The present-day temple, which began taking form in the 10th century, emerged on the hallowed grounds of previous sacred structures within the complex. This ambitious project was inaugurated by Anantavarman Chodaganga, the inaugural ruler of the Eastern Ganga dynasty.

Famous Temples with High Footfall

#1 Tirumala Venkateswara Temple

The Tirumala Venkateswara temple, dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu and renowned as the wealthiest deity, stands as one of India's busiest pilgrimage sites, attracting over 60,000 devotees daily.

#2 Vaishno Devi Temple

This temple of Vaishno Devi is situated high up in the mountains and is dedicated to Adi Shakti. It is perched on the mighty Trikuta Hill in Jammu. The shrine attracts a huge number of devotes on a daily basis. People usually take up a trek to walk to the top of the hill.

#3 Jagannath Temple

The Jagannath Temple in Puri, one of the "Char Dhams" of Hindu pilgrimage, holds the promise of salvation. Within its sacred precincts reside Lord Jagannath, along with the divine presences of Goddess Subhadra and Balabhadra.

#4 Amarnath Dham

Perched amidst the lofty peaks of Jammu and Kashmir, Amarnath Dham stands as one of Hinduism's four sacred pilgrimage sites. This remarkable place resembles a cave and is renowned for the naturally formed lingam, representing Lord Shiva, which manifests anew every year.

#5 Badrinath Temple

Nestled within the grandeur of the Himalayan Range, the Badrinath Temple is a sacred abode dedicated to Lord Vishnu, affectionately known as Badrinathji. It is said that Lord Vishnu sought refuge here for meditation, recognizing his growing entanglement in worldly matters.

#6 Dwarakadheesh Temple

Situated in Gujarat, the Dwarakadeesh Temple is a divine sanctuary devoted to Lord Krishna. It is believed that the temple's original foundations were laid by Lord Krishna's grandson, Vajranabha. This sacred site was later included in the revered "Char Dhams" during the 8th century.
